Timely and Relevant: Understanding Different Types of Wildlife Intrusions

Wildlife control in Alpharetta, GA, often involves addressing specific types of animals. Each species presents unique challenges and requires specialized knowledge and techniques for safe and effective removal and prevention. Understanding the behavior and habits of these animals is crucial for successful wildlife management.

Raccoons in the Attic

Raccoons are intelligent and adaptable creatures that can easily find their way into attics through damaged vents, chimneys, or gaps in the roofline. Once inside, they can cause significant damage to insulation, wiring, and ductwork. Raccoons can also carry diseases like rabies. Their activity often involves loud thumping and scurrying sounds, especially at night. Removing raccoons requires careful trapping and exclusion to prevent them from simply finding another way back in. We also address the damage they leave behind, including sanitation and repair of compromised areas. These animals are strong and can create sizable entry points in a short amount of time.

Squirrels in the Walls or Attic

Squirrels are common invaders, finding entry points through small gaps, vents, or even by chewing through wood. They often build nests in attics, walls, and chimneys, causing noise and potential damage. Their constant gnawing can damage electrical wires, posing a fire risk. Squirrel activity is typically heard during the day. Effective squirrel control involves identifying all entry points, trapping or excluding the squirrels, and then sealing the entry points. Proactive tree trimming can also help deter squirrels from accessing your roof. Squirrels are persistent and can squeeze through surprisingly small openings.

Bats in the Attic or Walls

Bats can enter homes through tiny cracks and crevices, roosting in attics, walls, and even chimneys. While beneficial for insect control, their droppings (guano) can accumulate, causing unpleasant odors and posing health risks, including histoplasmosis. Bat removal requires a specialized approach, typically involving exclusion devices that allow bats to leave but prevent re-entry. This is often done during specific times of the year to avoid trapping pups. Sealing all entry points after exclusion is critical to prevent future infestations. Bats require very small openings to gain access, making detailed inspections essential.

Opossums Under the Deck or in Crawl Spaces

Opossums are marsupials that often seek shelter under decks, porches, or in crawl spaces. They are attracted to food sources like garbage and pet food left outdoors. While generally not aggressive, they can carry diseases and create unpleasant odors with their urine and feces. Humane trapping and removal are effective for opossums. Securing garbage cans and removing outdoor food sources are important preventative measures. Opossums are opportunistic foragers and will exploit readily available food sources.

Snakes in the Yard or Home

Snakes can be alarming to encounter, whether in your yard or, less commonly, inside your home. While many snakes are harmless, some in the Alpharetta area can be venomous. We can safely identify and remove snakes, both venomous and non-venomous. Reducing potential hiding places, such as overgrown vegetation and piles of debris, can help deter snakes. We prioritize safe handling and removal for both the snake and the homeowner. Knowing which snakes are present in your area is important for understanding potential risks.

Birds Nesting in Vents or Eaves

Birds nesting in vents, chimneys, or under eaves can cause blockages, create a mess with droppings and nesting materials, and introduce parasites like bird mites. Removing nests should be done after the birds have left or with guidance from wildlife experts to ensure compliance with regulations protecting certain bird species. Installing bird netting or screens over vents and openings can prevent birds from nesting in undesirable locations. Bird nests can quickly accumulate and cause significant blockages.

Other Potential Wildlife Intruders

Depending on the season and local environment, other wildlife such as skunks, groundhogs (woodchucks), and even coyotes can pose issues for homeowners in Alpharetta. Each of these animals requires specific knowledge and techniques for safe and effective management. Our team stays updated on the behaviors and presence of various wildlife species in the region to provide appropriate solutions. Understanding the migratory patterns and breeding seasons of local wildlife is important for effective control measures.

Dealing with any type of wildlife intrusion requires a careful and knowledgeable approach.
